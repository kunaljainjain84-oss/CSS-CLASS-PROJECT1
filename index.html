ANIMALS

What are animals?

Animals are complex multicellular living organisms in the Kingdom Animalia, characterized by their ability to move, consume other living things (heterotrophy) for energy, respond to stimuli, and possess eukaryotic cells with a nucleus but lacking cell walls, differing from plants and fungi.

They exist in diverse forms across land, air, and water, from microscopic creatures to giant whales, playing vital roles in ecosystems and featuring unique traits like specialized tissues (e.g., muscles, nerves) and collagen.

Facts about animals

Animals have amazing adaptations, like octopuses with three hearts and blue blood, or flamingos forming "flamboyances," while some, like elephants, "hear" through their feet, and sea otters hold hands to stay together while sleeping.

Crows hold funerals, butterflies taste with their feet, and male seahorses give birth, showcasing unique survival strategies across the animal kingdom.

Unique Abilities & Anatomy

Octopuses: Have three hearts (two for gills, one for the body) and blue blood.

Elephants: Detect low-frequency sounds and vibrations through their feet.

Dolphins: Sleep with only half their brain at a time to stay alert.

Frogs: Use their eyeballs to push food down their throat.

Butterflies: Taste with their feet.
